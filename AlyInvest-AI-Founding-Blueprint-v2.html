Section 01

Executive Summary & Product Philosophy

AlyInvest AI is a premium AI wealth advisor that sits above any brokerage. It is not a trading platform, not a brokerage, and not a robo-advisor that holds a book of assets. It never holds money, never executes trades, never moves assets, and never asks the user to move assets. Users keep investing exactly where they already do — through Trading 212, Interactive Brokers, Robinhood, eToro, Charles Schwab, Fidelity, or Vanguard — and AlyInvest reads that portfolio, analyzes it, and advises on it. The product is the advice and the relationship around it, not custody and not execution.

This is a deliberate structural choice, not a limitation. By refusing custody and execution, AlyInvest stays on the analysis-and-advice side of the line, avoids the operational and regulatory surface area of a broker-dealer, and — most importantly — is free to give genuinely aligned guidance because it earns nothing from order flow, spreads, or trade volume. Its only incentive is that the advice is good enough that the user keeps paying for it, year after year.

1.1 The advisor philosophy: a hired professional, not a chatbot

The single experiential goal is this: the user should never feel they are talking to ChatGPT with a finance skin. They should feel they hired a professional wealth manager who happens to be always available. A general chatbot answers whatever you ask, forgets you the moment the tab closes, and treats every question as if it were the first. A wealth manager does the opposite — and so does AlyInvest.

Proactive

It reaches out first. It flags rising concentration, drift from stated goals, an upcoming earnings date on a holding, a missed monthly contribution — before the user thinks to ask. It only speaks when the change is meaningful.

Remembers everything

It knows the user's goals, risk profile, prior recommendations, and — critically — why a recommendation changed since last time. It never re-litigates settled context or contradicts itself between sessions.

Grows with the user

Every interaction, every piece of feedback, every portfolio change deepens its understanding. Year three is materially more personalized than month one, and the user can feel the difference.

Understands their style

It learns whether the user is a patient buy-and-hold investor or an active tinkerer, whether they favor dividends or growth, and adapts tone and guidance accordingly — while letting the user review and edit what it has learned.

Concretely, that means advice is never generic. Two users asking the identical question — “Should I buy this technology company?” — should not get the identical answer. A user already 35% weighted to technology is told to avoid increasing exposure, because the marginal risk is concentration, not opportunity. A user holding only broad ETFs is told that adding a single quality technology name could improve growth potential and diversify their return drivers. Same stock, same market data, same engine — opposite recommendation, because the recommendation is about the person and their portfolio, not about the stock in isolation.

1.2 The moat is the relationship, not the model

Core moat. The defensible asset is not the AI model. Any competitor can call the same LLMs tomorrow. The moat is the years-long relationship AlyInvest builds with each user — encoded in two permanent, cumulative systems that a competitor cannot recreate on day one no matter how good their model is: the Investment Memory and the Investment Timeline.

A model is a commodity input. A five-year record of a specific user's goals, risk evolution, every recommendation ever made to them, the reasoning behind each one, how their portfolio and health scores tracked over time, and how they responded to advice — that is not a commodity. It is switching cost and personalization compounding together. The longer someone stays, the better the advice, and the more painful it is to leave and start from zero elsewhere. Time is the competitor's disadvantage and our asset.

  • Investment Memory — a living profile: goals, risk tolerance, preferences, preferred sectors, horizon, monthly investing habits, past recommendations and explanations, user feedback, portfolio and allocation history, historical health scores, and confidence history. It personalizes every future answer, and the user can inspect and correct it.
  • Investment Timeline — an append-only, permanent record of every recommendation ever issued: date, portfolio snapshot, the recommendation, its confidence, its reasoning and assumptions, the supporting calculations, the news and fundamentals and risk metrics that shaped it, and the market conditions at the time. It is the auditable history of the user's investing journey and the substrate for the comparison-based “What Changed?” experience.

1.4 Founding principles (v2)

  • Never executes, never holds, never moves. No custody, no order routing, no asset movement — ever. We advise; the user acts through their own broker.
  • Brokers provide data only. Every integration is read-only and exists solely to feed the engine. New brokers are addable without touching the Financial Engine.
  • The engine owns every number. No LLM computes a value; the deterministic engine is the sole source of truth for all math.
  • Every recommendation is explainable — and compared to the last one. “What Changed?” is comparison-based: each recommendation explains what changed versus the previous one and why.

If a feature cannot be delivered as read-only analysis and advice, it does not ship. This posture is a product commitment and a regulatory boundary, not a temporary limitation.

Section 02

System Architecture & Data Flow

AlyInvest AI is a read-only wealth advisor that sits above the user's existing brokerage. The architecture is organized around one non-negotiable invariant: portfolio data flows in, advice flows out, and nothing in the system can ever move money, place an order, or mutate a holding. Every component described below is either a data source, a deterministic calculator, a reasoning layer, or a memory writer. There is no order-management component, no broker-write credential, and no execution queue anywhere in the topology — and this is enforced structurally, not merely by policy.

Architectural north star. The Python Financial Engine is the single source of truth for every number. GPT performs structured, personalized reasoning over engine outputs and Investment Memory. Claude (Opus 4.8, claude-opus-4-8) is the user-facing "Your Advisor" that writes premium plain-language explanations. The LLMs never compute a value, never invent data, and never promise a return. The relationship — Investment Memory + the append-only Investment Timeline — is the moat, so the persistence branch is a first-class citizen of the pipeline, not an afterthought.

2.1 The Canonical Pipeline

Every advisory interaction — a dashboard load, an "Ask Your Advisor" question, or a nightly proactive check — traverses the same ordered pipeline. Portfolio data originates from one of three sources (in strict priority order), is normalized by a read-only Connections Layer, enriched with market data, reduced to hard numbers by the Financial Engine, contextualized against the user's history, reasoned over by GPT, narrated by Claude, and delivered to the UI or as a notification. A parallel persistent branch writes to Investment Memory and appends to the Investment Timeline.

There is no execution path in this diagram, and there cannot be one. The Connections Layer holds only read-scoped brokerage tokens (or ingests CSV / manual data), so even a fully compromised reasoning layer has no capability to trade. The furthest-right box the system can reach on the user's behalf is "deliver a notification". Any request the user phrases as "do it for me / rebalance / sell X" is answered with instructions the user executes at their broker — never an action AlyInvest takes.

2.2 The Financial Engine Is a Fact Barrier

The single most important structural decision is that GPT and Claude receive facts only as a validated, engine-produced JSON envelope. They are never handed raw prices, raw positions, or free-form market text and asked to do arithmetic. This makes hallucinated numbers impossible by construction: any figure that appears in advice must have a corresponding key in the engine envelope, and the rendering layer rejects LLM output that introduces a numeric claim not traceable to that envelope.

2.3 Deployment Topology

The system is a set of stateless HTTP services plus asynchronous workers, coordinated by Redis and backed by PostgreSQL and object storage. Broker adapters are the only components that hold third-party credentials, and those credentials are read-only.

Next.js / React Web

The dashboard, "Your Advisor" thread, Investment Timeline browser, and Memory editor. Server components fetch through the gateway; no direct DB or broker access. Renders engine numbers verbatim and advisor prose as delivered.

FastAPI Gateway

Single public API surface. Handles auth (Supabase/Clerk JWT verification), request validation, rate limiting, and orchestration of the synchronous advisory path. Stateless and horizontally scaled behind a load balancer.

Connections / Ingestion Workers

Per-broker adapters (Trading 212, Interactive Brokers, Robinhood, eToro, Schwab, Fidelity, Vanguard), plus CSV and manual pipelines. Normalize to canonical positions. Read-only scopes only. Adding a broker never touches the Financial Engine.

Market Data Gateway

Fan-in for quotes, fundamentals, corporate actions, and news. Vendor-abstracted; every fetch produces an immutable market_data_snapshot so engine runs are reproducible. Aggressively cached in Redis.

Python Financial Engine

The deterministic calculator. Runs as an in-process library inside the gateway and workers (see 2.5). Emits the fact envelope. No I/O of its own — it receives normalized positions + a market snapshot and returns numbers.

Memory Service

Owns Investment Memory: goals, risk profile, preferences, sectors, horizon, monthly-investment habits, prior recommendations/explanations, feedback, allocation & health-score history, confidence history. Exposes read (context assembly) and write (learning) APIs.

Timeline Store

Append-only service over the time-partitioned timeline_entries table. Writes are immutable; the only mutation is soft-redaction for privacy. Serves the "What Changed?" diff by fetching the previous entry for the same portfolio.

Notification Service

Consumes "meaningful change" events from the monitoring workers, dedupes/throttles them, and dispatches push + email. Never fires on noise — only on the meaningful-change taxonomy (2.6).

Scheduler

Enqueues nightly and intraday monitoring jobs per user (market-close sweeps, earnings/dividend calendar checks, monthly-target checks). Fan-out is one job per portfolio onto the worker queue.

Redis

Market-data cache, engine-output cache (keyed by portfolio hash + snapshot ID), job queue (broker sync, monitoring, memory/timeline writes), and rate-limit counters.

PostgreSQL + time-partitioned history

System of record. Users, connections (read-only token references), canonical positions, Investment Memory, and the monthly-partitioned timeline_entries / allocation_history / health_score_history tables.

Object Storage (Supabase)

Large, immutable artifacts: full portfolio snapshots referenced by each timeline entry, uploaded CSV files, and supporting-calculation blobs too large for a row. Timeline rows store the object key, not the payload.

2.5 Engine Invocation: In-Process Library, Not a Service

The Financial Engine is invoked as an in-process Python library, imported directly by the FastAPI gateway (for the synchronous path) and by the monitoring workers (for the async path). It is deliberately not a separate microservice.

Why in-process

The engine is pure and CPU-bound with no I/O; a network hop would add latency and a failure mode for zero benefit. Callers already run Python, so there is no language boundary. Determinism and testability are preserved either way — a service wrapper would only add operational surface.

Isolation without a network

The engine module accepts only two inputs — normalized positions and a market-data snapshot — and returns the fact envelope. It performs no I/O and imports no broker or LLM code, so "no execution path" is enforced at the module boundary, not by network policy.

2.6 Synchronous Path vs. Asynchronous Path

Two distinct request lifecycles share the same pipeline stages but run under different latency and concurrency budgets.

Synchronous path — dashboard load & "Ask Your Advisor"

  1. Gateway verifies the JWT and loads the user's canonical positions (from the freshest cached broker sync) and Investment Memory context.
  2. Market Data Gateway returns a snapshot (cache hit in the common case); the engine runs in-process and produces the fact envelope.
  3. GPT is called with the envelope + assembled memory context and returns a schema-validated structured recommendation.
  4. Claude (Opus 4.8, streaming) turns the structured recommendation into advisor prose; tokens stream to the UI so the user sees the response forming.
  5. After the prose finalizes, the persistence branch fires asynchronously — the user is not blocked on the Memory/Timeline write.

Target: interactive latency. Streaming Claude keeps perceived latency low even when adaptive thinking runs longer on a complex question.

Asynchronous path — daily monitoring, notifications, persistence writes

  1. The Scheduler fans out one monitoring job per portfolio onto the Redis queue (per-user fan-out — see 2.7).
  2. Each worker re-runs the Connections sync (read-only), the market snapshot, and the engine, then diffs the new fact envelope against the previous Timeline entry for that portfolio.
  3. A change is emitted only if it clears the meaningful-change bar: concentration too high, risk increasing, diversification decreasing, upcoming earnings on a held name, dividend announcement, material news on an owned company, drift from stated goals, a missed monthly-investment target, or a large valuation change. Sub-threshold deltas produce no event.
  4. Meaningful events go to the Notification Service (dedup + throttle → push/email). Every monitoring run — event or not — appends a Timeline entry and updates Memory histories.

"What Changed?" is comparison-based, not regenerated. Because the async path always writes an immutable Timeline entry, each recommendation can be diffed against its predecessor: which numbers moved, which assumptions changed, which market events (earnings, dividends, news, macro) drove it, and whether the user's own portfolio changed. The system never regenerates a fresh daily explanation from scratch — it explains the delta, which is what makes the advisor feel like it remembers.

2.7 Caching & Horizontal Scaling

Market-data cache

Quotes, fundamentals, and news are cached in Redis keyed by symbol + freshness class. A single close-of-day snapshot serves an entire monitoring sweep, so thousands of portfolios reuse one set of fetches.

Engine-output cache

The fact envelope is cached under hash(canonical_positions) + snapshot_id. Because the engine is deterministic, an unchanged portfolio against an unchanged snapshot is a pure cache hit — no recomputation, and identical numbers guaranteed.

Stateless API, horizontal scale

The gateway holds no per-user state between requests (session lives in the JWT; data lives in Postgres/Redis), so it scales by adding identical replicas behind the load balancer.

Per-user worker fan-out

Monitoring is embarrassingly parallel: one queue job per portfolio. Worker count scales with user count independently of the API tier. A slow broker or a heavy portfolio delays only its own job, never the interactive path.

The two tiers scale on independent axes: API replicas track concurrent interactive load (dashboard + advisor traffic), while worker replicas track total portfolio count (the nightly sweep). Neither the LLM latency of the sync path nor the volume of the async sweep can starve the other, because they draw from separate pools and never share a request thread.

Section 03 · New pillar

Portfolio Connections Layer

The Portfolio Connections Layer is the only way portfolio data enters AlyInvest. It is a self-contained ingestion boundary that sits entirely outside the Financial Engine: it acquires holdings from whatever source the user has, normalizes every record into one internal shape, stamps each record with provenance, and hands a clean, versioned snapshot to the engine. The engine never learns which broker the data came from, never calls a broker SDK, and never sees a raw API payload. Adding Fidelity tomorrow is a new adapter file; it is never a change to the engine.

Core principle. Connectors provide data; the engine consumes a normalized snapshot; the AI analyzes that snapshot. These are three separate layers with a one-directional flow. Because the layers are decoupled by a normalized contract, brokers are pluggable and the engine is source-agnostic.

3.1 Source priority order and rationale

A user's holdings may be reachable through more than one source at once (e.g. a Trading 212 API connection and a legacy CSV they uploaded last year). When sources overlap for the same account, the layer resolves to a single authoritative record set using a fixed priority ladder:

PrioritySourceWhy it winsProvenance stamp
1Official brokerage API (OAuth, read-only)Authoritative, live, reconciled by the broker itself; positions, cost basis, cash, and transactions are exact and time-stamped by the custodian of record.source = "broker_api:<provider>"
2CSV portfolio importUser-supplied export from a broker; accurate at the moment of export but static and unverifiable against the live account. Used when no API exists (Vanguard/Fidelity retail, some regions) or the user declines to connect.source = "csv_import"
3Manual entryLowest fidelity; typed by the user. Necessary for assets no broker exposes (private holdings, crypto wallets) or for users who want to try the product before connecting anything.source = "manual"

Priority is applied per account and per position key, not globally: a user can have an API-connected ISA and a manually entered pension in the same portfolio, each retaining its own source. Only when two sources describe the same account does the higher-priority source shadow the lower one, and the shadowed record is retained (not deleted) so the Investment Timeline can explain a later source upgrade ("your Trading 212 API connection replaced the CSV you imported in March").

Higher priority means more trusted provenance, not "newer". A live API snapshot from 30 seconds ago beats a CSV from today because the API is continuously reconcilable; but staleness still lowers confidence (see 3.7) regardless of tier.

3.2 The provider-agnostic PortfolioConnector interface

Every source — real broker, CSV, or manual — implements the same interface. This is the seam that keeps the engine broker-blind. All methods are read/query only; there is deliberately no place_order, transfer, or withdraw anywhere in the type. The interface is architecturally incapable of mutating a broker account.

from abc import ABC, abstractmethod
from datetime import datetime
from typing import Optional

class PortfolioConnector(ABC):
    """Read-only ingestion boundary. Providers PROVIDE data; they never execute.
    Every returned record carries provenance (source + as_of + quality + connection_id)."""

    provider: str          # e.g. "trading212", "csv_import", "manual"
    read_only: bool = True # invariant; enforced at construction, never overridden

    @abstractmethod
    def authorize(self, user_id: str) -> "AuthChallenge":
        """Begin OAuth (read-only scopes ONLY) or return a no-op for csv/manual.
        Returns the redirect/authorization challenge; never stores raw tokens here."""

    @abstractmethod
    def connect(self, user_id: str, grant: "AuthGrant") -> "ConnectionAccount":
        """Exchange the grant for a connection. Tokens are written to the secrets
        vault by reference only; this method returns metadata, never a token."""

    @abstractmethod
    def fetch_positions(self, connection_id: str) -> list["Position"]:
        """Current holdings, normalized. Missing fields are marked MISSING, never guessed."""

    @abstractmethod
    def fetch_transactions(
        self, connection_id: str, since: Optional[datetime] = None
    ) -> list["Transaction"]:
        """Trade/dividend/fee history, normalized. `since` enables incremental sync."""

    @abstractmethod
    def fetch_cash_balances(self, connection_id: str) -> list["CashBalance"]:
        """Un-invested cash per currency, normalized."""

    @abstractmethod
    def get_connection_status(self, connection_id: str) -> "ConnectionStatus":
        """CONNECTED | DEGRADED | EXPIRED | REVOKED | ERROR, with last_synced_at."""

    @abstractmethod
    def disconnect(self, connection_id: str) -> None:
        """Revoke tokens at the provider and purge them from the vault. Idempotent."""

3.3 The normalized internal model (what the engine actually sees)

Regardless of source, every connector returns these four shapes and nothing else. This is the contract the Financial Engine is written against. Provenance is not optional: source, as_of, quality, and connection_id are non-nullable on every data record so downstream layers can always answer "where did this number come from, which connection produced it, and how much do we trust it?"

One consistent provenance placement. The three data records — Position, Transaction, CashBalance — never carry connection_id as a loose top-level field. It lives, together with source/as_of/quality, inside a single embedded Provenance value reached via record.prov. ConnectionAccount is the one exception, and deliberately so: it is not a data record but the identity of the connection itself, so connection_id is its own top-level primary field there. The engine therefore reads provenance the same way for every position, transaction, and cash balance: record.prov.connection_id.

from dataclasses import dataclass
from datetime import datetime
from decimal import Decimal
from enum import Enum
from typing import Optional

class DataQuality(str, Enum):
    VERIFIED = "verified"   # from an authoritative source, complete
    DERIVED  = "derived"    # computed from other verified fields (e.g. mkt_value = qty*price)
    STALE    = "stale"      # last known good, past its freshness window
    MISSING  = "missing"    # field is absent; NEVER fabricated
    # NOTE: there is intentionally no SYNTHESIZED/ESTIMATED value here.

class AssetClass(str, Enum):
    EQUITY = "equity"; ETF = "etf"; FUND = "fund"; BOND = "bond"
    CRYPTO = "crypto"; CASH = "cash"; OTHER = "other"

@dataclass(frozen=True)
class Provenance:
    source: str          # "broker_api:trading212" | "csv_import" | "manual"
    as_of: datetime      # when THIS record was true at the source (UTC)
    quality: DataQuality
    connection_id: str   # the connection that produced this record

@dataclass(frozen=True)
class Position:
    symbol: str
    isin: Optional[str]
    asset_class: AssetClass
    quantity: Decimal
    avg_cost: Optional[Decimal]      # MISSING-tolerant: None + prov.quality=MISSING

    market_value: Optional[Decimal]  # DERIVED when quantity*market_price is computed here
    currency: str                    # ISO-4217
    prov: Provenance                 # connection_id lives here, not top-level

@dataclass(frozen=True)

3.5 Sync and reconciliation

Data is refreshed on two triggers: scheduled (a per-connection cron cadence — intraday for API sources, e.g. every 30–60 min during market hours; on-open/on-close otherwise) and on-demand (user pull-to-refresh, or immediately after a fresh connect/CSV import). CSV and manual sources have no live refresh; their "sync" is the import/edit event itself.

3.6 Resilience

  • OAuth token refresh. Access tokens are refreshed proactively before expiry using the stored refresh token (vault-held). On refresh failure the connection moves to EXPIRED and the user is prompted to re-authorize; stale data is retained and clearly labeled meanwhile.
  • Rate limits. Each adapter declares its provider's quota; a per-provider token-bucket limiter throttles calls so one user's refresh cannot starve another's. Incremental since fetches minimize call volume.
  • Retries and backoff. Transient errors (429/5xx/timeouts) retry with exponential backoff and jitter, capped; non-transient errors (401/403 scope) fail fast and flip status to EXPIRED/REVOKED.
  • Per-broker circuit breakers. A breaker per provider (states CLOSED → OPEN → HALF_OPEN) trips after a rolling failure threshold, so a Robinhood outage stops us from hammering Robinhood and does not affect Trading 212 syncs at all. While OPEN, syncs short-circuit to serve the last good snapshot.
  • Graceful degradation. When live data is unavailable, the portfolio still renders from the last snapshot, every affected record is marked STALE with its as_of, and the engine's confidence output is lowered accordingly. The product keeps working; it just tells the truth about freshness.

3.7 Data quality and the never-fabricate rule

Missing data is marked missing. The system never invents a value to fill a gap. A missing cost basis stays MISSING; a stale price stays STALE with its timestamp; an unmappable CSV row is quarantined. Fabricating inputs would poison every downstream calculation and every recommendation — so it is structurally forbidden, not merely discouraged.

Quality propagates: the connections layer attaches per-record DataQuality via prov.quality, and the Financial Engine lowers the confidence of any metric derived from STALE or MISSING inputs. The advisor then discloses this to the user ("cost basis for 2 holdings is unavailable, so return figures for those are omitted, not estimated"). Facts, analysis, assumptions, and uncertainty stay clearly separated all the way from ingestion to explanation.

3.8 Read-only, no-custody guarantee

The no-execution posture is enforced at multiple layers so it cannot be defeated by a single mistake:

  • Scope minimization. Adapters request only read scopes (read:positions, read:transactions, read:balances). Trading, order, transfer, and withdrawal scopes are never in any authorization request; the DB CHECK constraint in 3.3 rejects them even if code tried.
  • Interface incapacity. PortfolioConnector exposes no mutating method. There is no code path — anywhere — from AlyInvest to a broker order endpoint.
  • Secrets vault. Tokens live in a dedicated secrets vault (e.g. cloud KMS / Vault); application tables hold only an opaque vault_token_ref. Tokens are never logged, never returned to the client, and are purged on disconnect().
  • No money movement. AlyInvest holds no funds and has no funding rails; it is not a broker or custodian. Users invest through their own broker, always.

Guardrail. Broker integrations only PROVIDE data. AlyInvest never executes trades, never holds money, never moves assets, and never asks the user to move assets. The connection layer is read-only by construction — architecturally incapable of touching a user's positions or cash.

Section 04

The Financial Engine — The Single Source of Truth

The Financial Engine is the deterministic Python core that computes every number in AlyInvest AI. No language model ever produces a metric, a score, a valuation, or a probability. GPT reasons over the engine's structured output; Claude ("Your Advisor") narrates it in plain language. Both consume numbers — neither invents them. This separation is the single most important design commitment in the system: it is what makes a regulated-adjacent wealth advisor trustworthy, auditable, and reproducible.

Core principle. If a number appears anywhere in the product — a health score, a fair value, a Sharpe ratio, a concentration percentage, a confidence level — it was computed by the Financial Engine, stamped with an engine version, and persisted as an immutable snapshot. The LLM layer may select, explain, and contextualize these numbers, but it may never derive them.

4.1 Source-Agnostic by Construction

The pivot to an advisor-above-any-brokerage architecture makes one property of the engine decisive: it is completely unaware of where portfolio data came from. It does not know whether holdings arrived from an Interactive Brokers API, a Trading 212 CSV export, or manual entry. It receives a single normalized Portfolio object and normalized MarketData from the Portfolio Connections Layer, and it computes.

4.2 Design Principles

  • Pure functions. Every calculation is a deterministic function of its typed inputs. No hidden state, no clocks read mid-calculation, no globals. Given the same inputs, it returns the same outputs, forever.
  • No network calls inside calculations. The engine never fetches. Market data, fundamentals, and prices are injected as typed arguments by the caller. This guarantees reproducibility and makes every function trivially unit-testable.
  • Unit-tested against fixtures. Each module ships with golden-file fixtures (known inputs, hand-verified expected outputs). CI fails if any formula drifts. Fixtures double as executable documentation of the formulas.
  • Versioned and stamped. Every engine output carries an engine_version (semantic version of the calculation code) and an as_of data timestamp. A metric computed today can be reproduced bit-for-bit next year by pinning the version and replaying the stored inputs.
  • Typed everywhere. Inputs and outputs are pydantic / dataclass models. Malformed or missing data is rejected at the boundary, never silently coerced. Missing fundamentals degrade the confidence of dependent metrics rather than fabricating values.
  • Never fabricates. If an input is absent (e.g. no free-cash-flow history), the dependent metric is returned as None with a reason, and downstream confidence drops. The engine does not guess.

4.4 Valuation (valuation.py)

Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

The intrinsic value is the sum of explicitly forecast free cash flows over an N-year horizon, each discounted at the discount rate r (WACC), plus a discounted Gordon-growth terminal value:

EV = Σ_{t=1..N}  FCF_t / (1 + r)^t   +   TV / (1 + r)^N

# Gordon-growth terminal value (perpetuity growth g, with r > g):
TV = FCF_N * (1 + g) / (r - g)

# Equity value and per-share fair value:
equity_value = EV - net_debt
fair_value_per_share = equity_value / shares_outstanding

Fair (intrinsic) value is then compared to the live price to yield an upside/downside percentage. The engine hard-requires r > g; if violated it returns None with reason "terminal_growth_exceeds_discount_rate" rather than emitting a nonsensical (negative-denominator) value.

def max_drawdown(prices: list[float]) -> float:
    peak = prices[0]
    mdd = 0.0
    for p in prices:
        peak = max(peak, p)
        mdd = min(mdd, (p - peak) / peak)   # most-negative trough vs running peak
    return mdd   # e.g. -0.32 means a 32% peak-to-trough decline

4.8 Portfolio (portfolio.py)

Portfolio functions aggregate per-holding results into allocation, position sizing, and — critically for personalization — concentration measures.

Asset allocation & position sizing

Each holding's weight is w_i = market_value_i / total_portfolio_value. Allocation is these weights grouped by asset class (equity / ETF / bond / cash / other) and by sector.

Diversification via Herfindahl–Hirschman Index (HHI)

HHI is the sum of squared weights. It is computed twice — once across individual holdings and once across sectors:

HHI = Σ_i  w_i ** 2          # w_i are fractions summing to 1  → HHI ∈ (0, 1]

# Normalized diversification score (higher = more diversified), N = number of buckets:
diversification = (1 - HHI) / (1 - 1/N)     # → 0 (fully concentrated) .. 1 (perfectly even)

An equal-weight portfolio of N holdings has HHI = 1/N; a single-holding portfolio has HHI = 1. The normalized form makes the score comparable across portfolios of different sizes.

Sector concentration

Beyond the aggregate HHI, the engine emits the top-sector concentration — the single largest sector weight max_s(w_sector_s) — and the full sorted sector-weight vector. This is the measure that drives personalization and proactive notifications: it is precisely how the system knows User A is 35% technology (advise against adding more) while User B holds only broad ETFs (adding a technology name could improve growth potential).

Why two concentration views. HHI answers "how diversified overall?"; top-sector concentration answers "which specific exposure is dangerous right now?" The advisor and the notification engine consume the latter to phrase concrete, portfolio-specific guidance.

Section 05 · Core moat

The Investment Memory System

The Investment Memory System is the durable, versioned record of everything AlyInvest AI has learned about a user and their portfolio over time. It is not a cache and not a chat transcript: it is a structured, provenance-tracked, permanently retained knowledge base that grows richer with every advisory cycle. Any advisor can call the same GPT and Claude models; none of them can call this user's years of goals, risk assessments, portfolio snapshots, recommendations, feedback, and behavioral signals. That accumulated context is the moat.

The moat. The Investment Memory is the single most defensible asset in the platform: it is versioned (we can reconstruct exactly what the advisor believed on any past date), provenance-tracked (every learned fact carries a source and confidence), user-editable (the user reviews and corrects what we learned), and permanent (nothing meaningful is ever silently discarded). A competitor with the same models starts from zero context; AlyInvest starts from years of relationship.

(A) Purpose & Principles

Investment Memory exists so that the advisor behaves like a wealth manager who has known the client for years rather than a stateless assistant answering a fresh question. It supplies the personalization inputs described in the Personalization section and feeds the reasoning context consumed by the AI Orchestration layer. It stores learned understanding only; it never holds money, never executes trades, and never moves assets. Broker connections write read-only portfolio observations into it; the Financial Engine writes computed metrics; the advisor writes recommendations and their provenance.

Four principles govern every table in this section:

  • Nothing meaningful is ever silently lost. Corrections and updates create new versions; they never destroy prior state. The only deletion path is user-initiated erasure (Section F).
  • Everything is versioned so the advisor can answer “what did we believe then, and why did it change?” Every belief is temporally addressable. Given any past date we can reconstruct the exact profile, risk band, preferences, portfolio, and metrics that were current then.
  • Learned facts carry confidence and provenance and are user-reviewable and editable. A machine-inferred fact (“prefers dividend income”) is labeled as inferred, scored with a confidence, and surfaced to the user who may confirm, edit, or forget it. A user-stated fact is authoritative and pinned.
  • The memory personalizes future advice. Memory is not archival for its own sake; each field is chosen because it changes what the advisor recommends. Two users asking about the same stock get different answers because their memory differs.

(C) Versioning Strategy

No single versioning pattern fits all memory. We deliberately apply four distinct patterns, chosen per table by the nature of the data. Note in particular that the strict SCD2 quartet (valid_from, valid_to, is_current, version_no) is used only for slowly-changing beliefs; learned facts use a different, self-chaining pattern, and the high-volume metric series use plain append-only writes.

1 · SCD Type 2 (temporal)

For slowly-changing, singular beliefs: profile, goals, risk, preferences, contribution habits. Each row carries valid_from, valid_to, is_current, version_no. Updating a belief closes the current row (valid_to = now, is_current = false) and inserts a successor. History is fully queryable: “what was the risk band on 2024-11-03?”

2 · Versioned facts (status + supersede-chain)

For learned facts and behavioral signals. These are not strict SCD2 (no is_current/version_no). A row carries status ∈ {active, superseded, forgotten}, a forward superseded_by self-pointer, valid_from/valid_to, and a companion memory_fact_revisions audit trail. A partial unique index on WHERE status = 'active' enforces one live fact per key.

3 · Append-only + supersedes_id

For recommendations and explanations (defined in Section 6). Rows are immutable once written; a revised recommendation is a new row whose supersedes_id points at the prior one. This yields the comparison chain the “What Changed?” feature walks.

(D) The Schema

PostgreSQL DDL follows. UUID primary keys (gen_random_uuid()), timestamptz throughout, jsonb where the shape is evolving, foreign keys to users(id) with ON DELETE CASCADE on every user-scoped table (matching the platform-wide convention in Section 11 and the erasure guarantee in Section F), CHECK constraints on enumerable columns, and the indexes each access pattern requires. Enum-like columns use CHECK constraints (portable, cheap to evolve) rather than native ENUM types.

Single-column snapshot PK is load-bearing. portfolio_snapshots deliberately keeps a single-column uuid primary key and is not range-partitioned. Four inbound foreign keys depend on this — holding_snapshots.snapshot_id, allocation_history.snapshot_id, contribution_events.snapshot_id, and (Section 6) recommendations.portfolio_snapshot_id. A composite (id, as_of) PK from partitioning would invalidate all four single-column references. Snapshot volume is one row per observation (not per day per metric), so it does not require partitioning; the high-cardinality daily metric series are partitioned instead. Likewise, Section 6's recommendations keeps a single-column uuid PK so that confidence_history.recommendation_id and user_feedback can reference it.

(F) Retention

Retention encodes the moat directly: the relationship history is permanent, the high-volume daily time-series is tiered hot/cold, and only truly non-memory data (raw quotes, article bodies, secrets) is ephemeral. Right to be forgotten (GDPR / CCPA). Deletion is triggered only by user-initiated account closure. On an erasure request we run a purge workflow that (1) revokes and deletes vaulted broker tokens immediately, (2) hard-deletes or crypto-shreds all rows keyed by user_id across every memory and timeline table, and (3) removes the user's rows from any derived aggregates. Detached cold partitions are not reached by CASCADE. Foreign-key enforcement (and therefore ON DELETE CASCADE) does not propagate into partitions that have been DETACHed from the parent table and exported to Parquet. The erasure workflow must handle cold storage explicitly: for each detached monthly partition it either (a) re-attaches the partition, runs the keyed DELETE (or drops it if wholly the erased user's), and re-detaches, or (b) rewrites the corresponding Parquet object with the user's rows removed, or drops the object outright when it contains only that user. The workflow enumerates the archived-partition manifest for the user's date range so no cold rows survive. Where anonymized, non-re-identifiable derivatives are retained for model evaluation, all direct and quasi-identifiers are stripped and the linkage user_id is destroyed. A tombstone erasure-audit record (request id, timestamp, scope, operator, partitions purged) is kept for compliance. Legal hold overrides auto-partition archival but never blocks a lawful erasure beyond its statutory window; a held user's data is quarantined rather than served to the advisor.

Raw quotes are cache-only. If it cannot personalize advice and is not part of the permanent relationship record, it does not belong in the Investment Memory.

(G) How Memory Personalizes Advice

Each advisory cycle, an assembler builds a compact memory context object by reading the current SCD2 rows (profile, goals, risk band, preferences, contribution habit), the active memory_facts and behavioral_signals above a confidence threshold, the latest portfolio_snapshots row with its allocation and risk metrics, the trailing health and confidence series, recent user_feedback, and — critically — the immediately prior recommendation and its recommendation_reasoning (Section 6) so the advisor can reason about what changed. This object is serialized as structured JSON with provenance and confidence attached to every learned field, then passed to the AI Orchestration layer: GPT consumes it for structured reasoning and Claude consumes it to write the personalized, plain-language explanation as “Your Advisor”. Because the context differs per user, the same stock yields different advice — an already-tech-heavy user (say 35% technology) is told to avoid adding exposure, while an ETF-only user is told the same name could improve growth potential. The Financial Engine still computes every number; memory only shapes which analysis is relevant and how it is explained — the LLM never computes a value and never promises a return. See the Personalization and AI Orchestration sections for the full assembly and prompt contracts.

Section 06 · New pillar

The Investment Timeline

The Investment Timeline is the permanent, append-only record of the user's entire investing journey. Every recommendation the advisor ever issues is written here once and never mutated, never regenerated, never deleted (except on account closure). Where the Investment Memory holds the evolving, editable model of who the user is, the Timeline holds the immutable, chronological ledger of what the advisor said and why — at the exact moment it said it, with the exact numbers, news, and market conditions that drove the call. Together they are the moat: a competitor can license the same models tomorrow, but cannot reconstruct four years of one user's recommendation history, superseded rationale, and confidence evolution.

Core principle. Every recommendation is permanent and immutable once written. The Timeline is the user's investing memory — the append-only source of truth that lets the advisor say, with authority, "here is what I told you six months ago, here is why I've changed my mind, and here is exactly which number moved." No row in recommendations, recommendation_reasoning, recommendation_explanations, or timeline_events is ever updated in place. Correction, reversal, and refinement all happen by appending a new record that supersedes the old one.

6.1 Purpose — the permanent half of the moat

The advisor must feel like a professional wealth manager who remembers everything. That is impossible without a durable, queryable record that survives every session, every app restart, and every model upgrade. The Timeline delivers three capabilities no stateless LLM can:

  • Reconstruct history on demand. "What did you recommend for my NVDA position last quarter, and what was your confidence?" is a single indexed query, not a hallucination.
  • Ground the "What Changed?" comparison. Because the prior recommendation is stored verbatim — its calculations, assumptions, and influencing news — the advisor compares the new recommendation against the stored one rather than regenerating a fresh explanation each day (see §9, "What Changed?").
  • Feed the Investment Memory. Historical confidence, allocation snapshots, and portfolio-health scores that Memory summarizes are derived from Timeline records. The Timeline is upstream; Memory is a learned projection of it.

Regulated-adjacent guardrail. The Timeline is an evidentiary record. Every stored recommendation must be reproducible: it pins engine_version and model_version, references (never inlines) the deterministic Financial Engine's computed values, and separates facts (fundamental/risk metrics), analysis (the recommendation), assumptions (what the reasoning presumed), and uncertainty (confidence + probabilistic scenarios). The advisor never promises a return; scenarios are stored as probabilistic ranges, and the record makes clear they were scenarios. AlyInvest holds no custody, moves no assets, and executes nothing — a recommendation with action = 'reduce' is advice, and its status tracks what the user chose to do at their own broker, never an execution the platform performed.

6.2 The Recommendation Record

Every Timeline entry captures the complete decision context. 6.2.2 recommendations — the immutable core (append-only)

This is the spine of the Timeline. Rows are written once. The only "mutation" ever permitted is by another table's FK pointing at it (a superseding row), or a monotonic status transition recorded through the lifecycle path (§6.3), which itself writes a new immutable status-event row rather than overwriting the column history.

Continuity is keyed on an explicit subject — a generated column that collapses instrument, portfolio, and goal scopes into a single well-defined key. For instrument advice subject is the ticker/ISIN; for portfolio-level advice it is the literal 'PORTFOLIO'; for a goal it is the goal_key. This is the key used by the "active head" partial index, the supersedes chain, and recommendation_diffs.subject in §9 — so "active head per subject" is unambiguous across all three scopes.

Why immutability is enforced, not merely intended. A database trigger rejects any UPDATE to the analytical columns (portfolio_snapshot_id, scope, instrument, goal_key, action, confidence, horizon, engine_version, model_version, created_at). The generated subject follows instrument/goal_key and so is immutable by construction. Only status and supersedes_id may transition, and only in the append-safe directions the lifecycle allows. This makes "we changed a past recommendation" structurally impossible.

CREATE OR REPLACE FUNCTION reject_reco_core_mutation() RETURNS trigger AS $$
BEGIN
  IF NEW.portfolio_snapshot_id IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.portfolio_snapshot_id
     OR NEW.scope          IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.scope
     OR NEW.instrument     IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.instrument
     OR NEW.goal_key       IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.goal_key
     OR NEW.action         IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.action
     OR NEW.confidence     IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.confidence
     OR NEW.horizon        IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.horizon
     OR NEW.engine_version IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.engine_version
     OR NEW.model_version  IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.model_version
     OR NEW.created_at     IS DISTINCT FROM OLD.created_at THEN
    RAISE EXCEPTION 'recommendations core columns are immutable (id=%)', OLD.id;
  END IF;
  RETURN NEW;
END;
$$ LANGUAGE plpgsql;

CREATE TRIGGER trg_reco_immutable
  BEFORE UPDATE ON recommendations
  FOR EACH ROW EXECUTE FUNCTION reject_reco_core_mutation();

Terminal states are absorbing — no recommendation ever leaves them, because change is expressed by issuing a new recommendation that supersedes this one, not by editing it back to life.

6.3.1 supersedes_id chaining — reconstructing "why it changed"

The self-FK turns the flat table into a linked list per subject. Each new recommendation points its supersedes_id at the one it replaces; the uq_reco_supersedes unique index guarantees each recommendation is superseded by at most one successor, so the chain is unambiguous. Walking backward from the current active head reconstructs the full history — every prior stance, its confidence, and the reasoning/news that were true at the time.

-- Reconstruct the full history for one subject, newest -> oldest,
-- following the supersedes chain from the current active head.
-- $2 is the subject key: a ticker, a goal_key, or the literal 'PORTFOLIO'.
WITH RECURSIVE chain AS (
    SELECT r.*, 0 AS depth
    FROM recommendations r
    WHERE r.user_id = $1 AND r.subject = $2 AND r.status = 'active'
  UNION ALL
    SELECT r.*, c.depth + 1
    FROM recommendations r
    JOIN chain c ON r.id = c.supersedes_id
)
SELECT c.depth, c.created_at, c.action, c.confidence, c.status,
       rr.supporting_calculations, rr.assumptions, rr.market_conditions
FROM chain c
JOIN recommendation_reasoning rr ON rr.recommendation_id = c.id
ORDER BY c.depth;   -- depth 0 = current, ascending = further back in time

This chain is exactly what powers the comparison-based "What Changed?" feature (§9): given the current head (depth 0) and its predecessor (depth 1), the advisor diffs the two stored records — which calculations changed, which assumptions changed, whether the portfolio snapshot changed, and which news arrived between them — instead of regenerating a fresh explanation. The Timeline makes the comparison a lookup, not a re-derivation.

6.4 timeline_events — the unified event stream

Recommendations are the most important entries, but the user's journey includes contributions, portfolio changes, feedback, notifications, milestones, preference edits, and "what changed" moments. timeline_events is the single append-only, time-partitioned stream that unifies all of them into one scrollable narrative. Each event carries a thin, typed envelope plus a ref_type/ref_id pointer to the full record (e.g. a recommendation row) and a payload for denormalized display data, so the UI renders the journey without fanning out to every source table.

CREATE TABLE timeline_events (

Append-only, permanent, partitioned. timeline_events is never updated in place — a correction is a new event. ref_id is a uuid so it can hold the id of any uuid-keyed record it points at (recommendations, portfolio snapshots, notifications); it is a soft polymorphic pointer resolved together with ref_type (no hard FK), so no cross-partition FK is required. Range-partitioning by occurred_at (monthly) keeps the hot recent window small and cheap to scan while the full history remains online for the life of the account. The composite PK (id, occurred_at) satisfies Postgres's requirement that the partition key be part of the primary key; the ref_id type change does not affect it.

6.5 How the Timeline powers the product

The scrollable investing journey (UI)

The main "Journey" view is a reverse-chronological render of timeline_events for the user, filtered by importance for density control. A recommendation event expands to show its stored explanation (recommendation_explanations.body at the user's knowledge_level), its confidence, and a "What changed?" affordance that pulls the depth-0-vs-depth-1 diff from the supersedes chain. Contributions, milestones, and notifications interleave to make the record feel like a lived history rather than a log.

Grounding the advisor's memory of prior advice

When the user asks "why are you telling me to trim Apple now — you liked it before?", the advisor does not guess. It resolves the active recommendation for subject AAPL, walks the supersedes chain for the prior active record, and renders a grounded answer from the two stored explanations, their stored confidences, and the stored reasoning/news deltas. The prose is fresh; the facts are retrieved. This is what makes the advisor feel like it remembers its own reasoning, not just the user's data.

Personalization is retrieval, not invention

Because two users' Timelines differ, the same instrument yields different advice: User A's Timeline and current snapshot show 35% technology, so a new tech idea resolves to avoid_increasing_exposure; User B holds only ETFs, so the same idea resolves to add for growth potential. The Financial Engine computes the concentration numbers; the Timeline records the recommendation and its evidence; Memory summarizes the pattern. No number is invented, and no return is promised — confidence and probabilistic scenarios are stored as uncertainty, exactly as issued.

6.6 Retention & scale

Retention = permanent. Recommendations, reasoning, explanations, news links, and timeline events are never auto-deleted. They are erased only on account closure (and then completely, to honor deletion requests), or redacted in place when a specific news reference or PII field must be scrubbed — a redaction clears the sensitive content while preserving the immutable record's existence and timestamps. The permanence is the moat; expiring old recommendations would destroy the very history that makes the advisor irreplaceable.

  • Partitioning for scale. timeline_events is range-partitioned by month; a scheduled job pre-creates upcoming partitions and (optionally) moves partitions older than a threshold to cheaper storage while keeping them attached and queryable. The recommendations spine is partitioned by created_at year for the same reason; its indexes on (user_id, created_at DESC) and the partial WHERE status = 'active' index keep the two hottest access paths — "recent journey" and "current standing advice per subject" — fast regardless of total history size.
  • Snapshots are referenced, never duplicated. A recommendation stores a portfolio_snapshot_id, so a decade of daily recommendations against a slowly changing portfolio does not re-store the portfolio a decade of times.
  • Immutability enables caching. Because rendered explanations never change, they cache indefinitely; only the set of events grows.

The Timeline is therefore both the product's memory and its defensibility: an append-only, immutable, permanently retained ledger — keyed by uuid and by an explicit per-subject continuity key — that the deterministic Financial Engine feeds with numbers, the reasoning model annotates with structured evidence, and the claude-opus-4-8 advisor narrates in plain language — one record per moment, kept for the life of the relationship.

Section 07 · New pillar

The Personalized Advice Engine

Every incumbent robo-advisor answers the question “is NVDA a buy?” the same way for everyone. AlyInvest does not. The Personalized Advice Engine treats a recommendation as a function of the individual, not of the instrument alone. The instrument is one input; the person—their portfolio, concentration, risk band, horizon, goals, existing exposure, cash, and behavioral history—is the rest.

Core thesis. Two users asking about the same stock should not necessarily receive the same recommendation. Advice is f(instrument, individual_context). The instrument’s facts and fundamentals are identical for both users; the context and therefore the reasoning differ. This is what separates a wealth advisor from a stock-tip feed.

Read-only, no execution. Personalization shapes advice, never action. The engine advises avoid_increasing_exposure or a measured add; it never places, routes, or moves anything, and never asks the user to move assets. The user acts through their own broker if and when they choose.

7.3 How it works: engine computes the context, the model reasons over it

Personalization is a strict two-stage separation of powers. The engine produces every number, including the incremental effect of the action being considered. GPT then reasons over that fixed numeric context to produce a structured, personalized recommendation. GPT selects, weighs, and explains; it never computes a figure and never overrides one.

The decisive detail is the incremental effect. For a candidate add of the instrument at a representative sizing (default: the user’s typical monthly_contribution, or an explicit amount from the query), the engine computes the post-trade state deterministically. HHI is a fraction on (0, 1]—the same scale as the Financial Engine, Memory (risk_metric_history.hhi), and the orchestration engine_output.diversification.hhi—so a single number flows everywhere:

def incremental_effect(portfolio, instrument, add_amount):
    total_before = portfolio.total_value
    total_after  = total_before + add_amount

    sec = instrument.sector
    sector_val_before = portfolio.sector_value(sec)
    sector_w_before   = sector_val_before / total_before
    sector_w_after    = (sector_val_before + add_amount) / total_after

    name_w_before = portfolio.value_of(instrument.id) / total_before
    name_w_after  = (portfolio.value_of(instrument.id) + add_amount) / total_after

    # Herfindahl over sector weights; HHI = sum(w_i**2), fraction in (0, 1]
    hhi_before = herfindahl(portfolio.sector_weights())
    hhi_after  = herfindahl(portfolio.sector_weights_after(sec, add_amount))

    return {
        "add_amount": add_amount,
        "sector": sec,
        "sector_weight_before": round(sector_w_before, 4),
        "sector_weight_after":  round(sector_w_after, 4),
        "name_weight_before": round(name_w_before, 4),
        "name_weight_after":  round(name_w_after, 4),
        "hhi_before": round(hhi_before, 4),
        "hhi_after":  round(hhi_after, 4),
        "delta_hhi":  round(hhi_after - hhi_before, 4),
        "delta_risk_score": round(portfolio.risk_delta(instrument, add_amount), 3),
        "sector_target_max": RISK_BAND_LIMITS[portfolio.risk_band]["sector_max"],
        "funded_from_cash": add_amount <= portfolio.cash_value,
    }

What personalization does and does not change. It changes the context (which numbers are relevant to this user) and the reasoning (how those numbers are weighed for this person). It never changes the numbers themselves—hhi_after, sector_weight_after, and delta_risk_score are engine outputs and are byte-identical regardless of who is asking. The model may not manufacture a “0.35” or a “ceiling of 0.25”; both come from the engine and the RISK_BAND_LIMITS table in §7.2.

7.6 Suitability and the no-two-users-alike principle

Personalization is how AlyInvest operationalizes suitability: advice must fit the person receiving it. A recommendation that breaches the user’s own risk-band ceilings, worsens a concentration the engine already flagged, or drifts from a stated goal is by definition unsuitable—and the engine’s deterministic checks catch it before GPT ever writes a word. The result is auditable: every personalized recommendation can be traced to the exact context numbers and memory facts that produced it, and stored in the Timeline for continuity.

No two users alike. The same instrument, the same market, the same fundamentals—yet User A hears “avoid increasing exposure” and User B hears “a measured position could improve your growth potential.” The difference is not a different model or a different prompt; it is a different context, computed deterministically from each person’s portfolio and remembered over years. That is the relationship, and it is the moat.

Never a promise. Personalized advice remains probabilistic: “could improve growth potential” is a scenario framed to the individual’s context, never a guaranteed return. The engine supplies ranges and confidence; the advisor communicates them honestly and distinguishes fact from analysis from assumption.

The routing, resilience, and cost controls live in a thin Orchestrator that composes providers:

  • Model routing. Config maps roles to providers: reasoner => gpt-structured, advisor => claude-advisor (default claude-opus-4-8). A role can be re-pointed without touching call sites.

Server-side reconciliation and repair

The GPT JSON is never trusted on its face. After schema validation, a deterministic reconciler walks every numeric leaf — confidence_score is exempt (it is the model's own judgment, not an engine number), but every metric value, scenario probability, return-range bound, and evidence value must match the signed engine_output within a strict tolerance. Every cited name is looked up against the single canonical vocabulary.

def reconcile(rec: PersonalizedRecommendation, engine: EngineOutput) -> ReconcileResult:
    """Reject any recommendation that references or restates a number
    the engine did not produce. Non-numeric fields pass through; numbers
    must be provably engine-sourced. All names are canonical: they must
    belong to engine.metric_names()."""
    mismatches: list[Mismatch] = []

    # 1. Every cited evidence metric must exist in the canonical set and match exactly.
    for ev in rec.supporting_evidence:
        truth = engine.lookup(ev.metric_name)          # None if not a canonical engine metric
        if truth is None:
            mismatches.append(Mismatch(ev.metric_name, "unknown_metric", ev.metric_value))
        elif not _close(ev.metric_value, truth):        # abs/rel tolerance 1e-6
            mismatches.append(Mismatch(ev.metric_name, truth, ev.metric_value))

    # 2. Scenario probabilities and ranges must equal the engine's, set-for-set.
    if not engine.scenarios_match(rec.expected_scenarios):
        mismatches.append(Mismatch("expected_scenarios", engine.expected_scenarios, rec.expected_scenarios))

    # 3. Valuation metrics referenced must be canonical and engine-sourced.
    for m in rec.valuation_summary.referenced_metrics:
        truth = engine.lookup(m.metric_name)
        if truth is None or not _close(m.value, truth):
            mismatches.append(Mismatch(m.metric_name, truth, m.value))

    # 4. metrics_referenced must be a subset of the canonical exported names.
    unknown = set(rec.metrics_referenced) - engine.metric_names()
    if unknown:
        mismatches.append(Mismatch("metrics_referenced", "subset_of_engine", sorted(unknown)))

    return ReconcileResult(ok=not mismatches, mismatches=mismatches)

On failure the orchestrator issues one bounded repair call: it returns the mismatch list to GPT with an instruction to re-emit valid JSON using only the supplied engine numbers and canonical names. A second failure is terminal — the recommendation is not shown; the system falls back to the deterministic engine-only summary (see 8.8). We never quietly patch a bad number in code, because a wrong-but-plausible number is more dangerous than a visible failure in a regulated-adjacent product.

Section 09

The “What Changed?” Engine

Most AI products regenerate their answer from scratch on every request. That is exactly what makes them feel like a chatbot: yesterday's advice evaporates, today's advice appears fully formed, and the user has no idea whether anything actually moved or whether the model simply re-rolled the dice. A professional wealth manager does the opposite. When you sit down with them, they say "Last quarter I told you to hold Microsoft. Here is what has changed since then, and here is why I am now telling you something different." The "What Changed?" Engine makes AlyInvest behave like that manager. It is comparison-based, not regenerative: every recommendation is diffed against the previous recommendation for the same subject, pulled from the Investment Timeline, and the user-facing narrative is authored from that diff — never from a blank slate.

Core principle. A recommendation is never explained in isolation. It is explained as a transition from the last recommendation. The unit of explanation is the delta, not the state. If nothing material changed, the honest answer is "nothing changed" — and we say so, rather than manufacturing fresh-sounding prose to fill the screen.

9.1 The questions the engine must answer

Every "What Changed?" view answers a fixed battery of questions. These are not marketing copy — they are the dimensions the diff engine computes over, and each maps to a concrete comparison against the previous Timeline entry.

The "What Changed?" Engine adds no new source data — it only compares two immutable Timeline rows. This is why the moat compounds: the longer the Timeline, the more transitions we can reason over.

9.2 How the diff is computed

The engine is a deterministic Python routine that sits after the Financial Engine has produced the new metric snapshot and after GPT has produced the new structured recommendation object, but before Claude writes anything. Its inputs are two objects of identical shape — prev and new — and its output is a single structured RecommendationDiff. The LLM is not involved in producing the diff; it only consumes it.

Align snapshotsPair prev/new metric snapshots + portfolio snapshots by key
Diff dimensionsCompute deltas per dimension; apply surface materiality bands

Persist diffWrite one recommendation_diffs row linking prev → new

Two-tier materiality: surface band vs. notify band

The single most important behavior is suppression of noise. A price wiggle of 0.4% is not a reason to email a user. But "worth showing in the app" and "worth interrupting the user with a push notification" are two different bars, and conflating them is a design error. AlyInvest therefore uses two nested thresholds per shared condition:

  • Surface band (this engine). The lower bar. A dimension enters changed_dimensions — and therefore appears on the in-app "What Changed?" card — when its delta crosses the surface band. This is the gate that keeps the card honest without being twitchy.
  • Notify band (Section 10, Proactive Advisor). The higher bar. A notification only fires when the delta additionally crosses the stricter notify band. This keeps interruptions rare and meaningful.

Monotonic-nesting invariant. For every shared condition, notify_band ≥ surface_band. A notification can therefore never fire without the corresponding dimension already being present in that diff's changed_dimensions. This is the structural guarantee behind the Section 10.1 promise that each notification maps one-to-one onto a What-Changed dimension: if a notification claims something changed, the persisted diff already contains matching evidence (the same dimension, the same computed delta, the same driver). The reverse does not hold — the app surfaces changes at the lower band that are deliberately kept below the notification threshold. In-app is chattier by design; notifications are quiet by design; the two never contradict each other.

Worked reconciliation. Consider a single sector sitting at 27% that just crossed the 25% watch line. Under the surface band this is a material diversification event: the card shows "technology crossed your 25% concentration watch line — now 27%." Under the notify band it is not yet a concentration_high notification, because 27% is below the 30% trigger. The user sees the drift building in-app; we do not interrupt them until it becomes a genuine breach. Both statements are true simultaneously precisely because notify_band (30%) ≥ surface_band (25%) and notify_band (0.05 HHI) ≥ surface_band (0.02 HHI). The thresholds no longer disagree — they are two rungs of one ladder.

from dataclasses import dataclass, field
from datetime import datetime
from decimal import Decimal

# SURFACE bands (this engine): the lower bar that puts a dimension on the in-app card.
# The Financial Engine owns the numbers; this table only decides what is worth surfacing.
METRIC_BANDS = {
    "fair_value":            Decimal("0.05"),
    "price_to_fair_value":   Decimal("0.05"),
    "pe_ratio":              Decimal("0.10"),
    "ev_ebitda":             Decimal("0.10"),
    "risk_score":            Decimal("0.05"),   # absolute, 0..1
    "portfolio_hhi":         Decimal("0.02"),   # absolute
    "expected_annual_vol":   Decimal("0.03"),   # absolute
}
SECTOR_WATCH_LINE = Decimal("0.25")             # surface: any sector crossing 25%

# NOTIFY bands (Section 10): the strictly-higher bar. INVARIANT: notify >= surface for
# every shared key, so a fired notification always has a matching surfaced dimension.
NOTIFY_HHI_DELTA       = Decimal("0.05")        # diversification_decreasing
SECTOR_CONCENTRATION   = Decimal("0.30")        # concentration_high (and rose >= 3pp)
SECTOR_CONCENTRATION_RISE = Decimal("0.03")

class MetricDelta:
    metric: str
    prev: Decimal | None
    new: Decimal | None
    abs_delta: Decimal
    pct_delta: Decimal | None
    crossed_threshold: bool          # e.g. price crossed fair value
    material: bool                   # against the SURFACE band

@dataclass
class RecommendationDiff:
    prev_recommendation_id: str
    new_recommendation_id: str
    subject: str                     # e.g. "MSFT" or "PORTFOLIO"
    action_from: str                 # "hold"
    action_to: str                   # "increase"
    action_changed: bool
    confidence_from: Decimal
    confidence_to: Decimal
    changed_dimensions: list[str] = field(default_factory=list)
    diff: dict = field(default_factory=dict)      # full structured delta, per dimension

def compute_diff(prev: "Recommendation", new: "Recommendation") -> RecommendationDiff:
    d = RecommendationDiff(
        prev_recommendation_id=prev.id,
        new_recommendation_id=new.id,
        subject=new.subject,
        action_from=prev.action,
        action_to=new.action,
        action_changed=(prev.action != new.action
                        or prev.action_strength != new.action_strength),
        confidence_from=prev.confidence,
        confidence_to=new.confidence,
    )

    # 1. calculations: numeric snapshot deltas, gated by per-metric SURFACE band.
    calc = _diff_metrics(prev.metric_snapshot, new.metric_snapshot)
    if any(m.material for m in calc):
        d.changed_dimensions.append("calculations")
        d.diff["calculations"] = [m.__dict__ for m in calc if m.material]

    # 2. assumptions: set diff over the structured assumption inputs.
    assump = _diff_dict(prev.assumptions, new.assumptions)
    if assump:
        d.changed_dimensions.append("assumptions")
        d.diff["assumptions"] = assump

    # 3. portfolio_changes: from the connections sync snapshot (read-only).
    port = _diff_holdings(prev.portfolio_snapshot, new.portfolio_snapshot)
    if port["added"] or port["removed"] or port["reweighted"]:
        d.changed_dimensions.append("portfolio_changes")
        d.diff["portfolio_changes"] = port

    # 4. diversification: HHI + sector concentration, both at the SURFACE band.
    #    We also stamp notify-eligibility so Section 10 never re-derives a number.
    div = _diff_diversification(prev.metric_snapshot, new.metric_snapshot)
    if div["hhi_delta_material"] or div["concentration_crossing"]:
        d.changed_dimensions.append("diversification")
        d.diff["diversification"] = div

    # 5. valuation / 6. earnings / 7. macro / 8. market_events
    for dim, fn in (("valuation", _diff_valuation),
                    ("earnings", _diff_earnings),
                    ("macro", _diff_macro),
                    ("market_events", _diff_events)):
        result = fn(prev, new)
        if result and result.get("material"):
            d.changed_dimensions.append(dim)
            d.diff[dim] = result

    # 9. rank causal drivers: which material dimensions best explain the action move.
    d.drivers = _rank_drivers(d, new)
    return d


def _diff_diversification(prev_snap, new_snap) -> dict:
    """Compute HHI + sector-concentration deltas and stamp BOTH tiers.
    Section 10 reads notify_eligible; it never recomputes the delta itself."""
    hhi_delta = abs(new_snap.portfolio_hhi - prev_snap.portfolio_hhi)
    top_sector, top_weight = new_snap.max_sector()          # (name, weight)
    prev_weight = prev_snap.sector_weight(top_sector)
    rise = top_weight - prev_weight
    crossed_watch = (prev_weight < SECTOR_WATCH_LINE <= top_weight)
    return {
        "hhi_from": prev_snap.portfolio_hhi,
        "hhi_to": new_snap.portfolio_hhi,
        "hhi_delta": hhi_delta,
        "hhi_delta_material": hhi_delta >= METRIC_BANDS["portfolio_hhi"],  # surface 0.02
        "top_sector": top_sector,
        "top_sector_weight": top_weight,
        "concentration_crossing": crossed_watch,             # surface: crossed 25%
        # notify eligibility (Section 10) -- strictly higher bar, computed once, here:
        "notify_diversification": hhi_delta >= NOTIFY_HHI_DELTA,          # 0.05
        "notify_concentration": (top_weight > SECTOR_CONCENTRATION        # 30%
                                 and rise >= SECTOR_CONCENTRATION_RISE),  # +3pp
    }

Guardrail: the LLM never computes the delta. Every number in the diff — every abs_delta, pct_delta, HHI change, threshold crossing, and both the surface- and notify-tier flags — is produced by deterministic Python over persisted snapshots. Claude receives the finished RecommendationDiff as read-only input. If a diff value is not present, Claude may not invent it, and it may not describe a change the diff does not contain. Section 10 likewise reads the pre-stamped notify_* flags rather than recomputing any threshold, so the two engines can never drift into disagreement.

Driver ranking

_rank_drivers is where the engine earns its keep. A raw list of eight changed dimensions is not an explanation. The driver ranker attributes the action change to the material dimensions with the largest normalized magnitude, so the narrative can lead with the real cause. Each driver is { dimension, direction, magnitude, human_label }. For a Hold → Increase on MSFT, the ranked drivers might be valuation gap widened (primary), then earnings beat (secondary), while macro is present in changed_dimensions but ranked low and therefore mentioned only in passing.

9.3 Schema: recommendation_diffs

Each diff is persisted as exactly one row, immutably linking the previous and new Timeline entries. Identifiers are consistent with Section 8: recommendations.id is the Timeline entry key, and both FKs point at it. The diff row is written in the same transaction as the new recommendation, so the Timeline and its diffs never drift.

CREATE TABLE recommendation_diffs (
    id                        UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    user_id                   UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES users(id),
    subject                   TEXT NOT NULL,            -- 'MSFT' | 'PORTFOLIO' | ...

    -- Link into the Investment Timeline (Section 8). Both reference recommendations.id.
    prev_recommendation_id    UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES recommendations(id),
    new_recommendation_id     UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES recommendations(id),

    -- Headline transition, denormalized for fast card rendering + notification checks.
    action_from               TEXT NOT NULL,            -- 'hold'
    action_to                 TEXT NOT NULL,            -- 'increase'
    action_changed            BOOLEAN NOT NULL,
    confidence_from           NUMERIC(4,3) NOT NULL,
    confidence_to             NUMERIC(4,3) NOT NULL,

    -- Which dimensions crossed the SURFACE band. Empty array == "nothing surfaced".
    changed_dimensions        TEXT[] NOT NULL DEFAULT '{}',

    -- Full structured delta, keyed by dimension (calculations, assumptions,
    -- market_events, portfolio_changes, diversification, valuation, earnings, macro).
    -- Diversification carries pre-stamped notify_* flags for Section 10 to consume.
    diff                      JSONB NOT NULL DEFAULT '{}'::jsonb,

    -- Ranked causal attribution: [{dimension, direction, magnitude, human_label}, ...].
    drivers                   JSONB NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]'::jsonb,

    -- SURFACE materiality: did anything reach the in-app card? (>= surface band)
    is_material               BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
    -- NOTIFY materiality: did any dimension additionally clear the stricter notify band?
    -- INVARIANT: is_notifiable IMPLIES is_material (notify band >= surface band).
    is_notifiable             BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
    -- Dimensions that cleared the notify band (subset of changed_dimensions).
    notify_dimensions         TEXT[] NOT NULL DEFAULT '{}',

    narrative                 TEXT,                     -- Claude's comparison narrative
    narrative_model           TEXT,                     -- provenance, e.g. 'claude-*'

    created_at                TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),

    CONSTRAINT diff_links_distinct  CHECK (prev_recommendation_id <> new_recommendation_id),
    CONSTRAINT diff_unique_per_new  UNIQUE (new_recommendation_id),  -- one diff per Timeline entry
    -- Enforce the monotonic-nesting invariant at the database level.
    CONSTRAINT diff_notify_implies_material CHECK (NOT is_notifiable OR is_material),
    CONSTRAINT diff_notify_subset CHECK (notify_dimensions <@ changed_dimensions)
);

CREATE INDEX idx_diffs_user_subject_time
    ON recommendation_diffs (user_id, subject, created_at DESC);

-- Fast "show me the meaningful changes" in-app feed (surface tier).
CREATE INDEX idx_diffs_material
    ON recommendation_diffs (user_id, created_at DESC)
    WHERE is_material;

-- Notification queue (strict notify tier) -- Section 10 subscribes to this.
CREATE INDEX idx_diffs_notifiable
    ON recommendation_diffs (user_id, created_at DESC)
    WHERE is_notifiable;

is_material := (cardinality(changed_dimensions) > 0) and is_notifiable := (cardinality(notify_dimensions) > 0). Because notify_dimensions ⊂ changed_dimensions (enforced by diff_notify_subset), is_notifiable can never be true unless is_material is true — the invariant is guaranteed by the schema, not merely by convention. When is_material is FALSE, no card content and no notification fire, and the "What Changed?" card renders a calm "No material change since <date>" state. When is_material is true but is_notifiable is false, the change is shown in-app and no push is sent. Silence and quiet-visibility are both first-class, queryable outcomes.

9.6 Ties to the Timeline and to Notifications

  • Investment Timeline (Section 8) is the sole source of the "previous." The engine never caches a shadow copy of prior advice; it reads the last recommendations row for the (user, subject) pair. Because Timeline entries are immutable and permanent, every diff is reproducible for the life of the account — and the chain of diffs is the story of how the advisor's thinking evolved, which is the relationship moat competitors cannot instantly recreate.
  • Notifications (Section 10, Proactive Advisor) consume is_notifiable and notify_dimensions. The proactive layer subscribes to the idx_diffs_notifiable feed and maps each notify dimension to its trigger: diversification → concentration_high / diversification_decreasing, earnings reported, valuation gap crossing, action change, portfolio_changes from a sync. Because notify dimensions are a computed subset of surfaced dimensions, every notification is backed by matching evidence already sitting in the same diff row — the section that fires the alert and the section that renders the comparison read the same numbers. A diff with an empty notify_dimensions produces no notification by construction; the surface tier may still show it in-app.
  • The card renders directly from one recommendation_diffs row — headline transition from the denormalized columns, body from drivers and diff, personalization from Investment Memory. No recomputation at render time, so history, its explanation, and any notification derived from it are always consistent.
Section 10 · New pillar

Proactive Advisor & Notification System

A wealth manager who calls you every day is not a wealth manager — it is spam. AlyInvest AI's proactive advisor is built on the opposite instinct: it monitors the portfolio, the market, and the user's own behavior continuously, but it speaks only when it has something genuinely meaningful to say. Every notification is the visible tip of a full recommendation, a What-Changed comparison, and an Investment Timeline entry that explains and justifies it. Nothing fires from a number the LLM invented; every threshold below is evaluated by the deterministic Financial Engine, and every message is written by Your Advisor against Engine-supplied facts. Any action stays entirely with the user, at their own broker.

Core principle — proactive but respectful. The advisor is always watching but rarely interrupting. A notification is a promise that a real, material change occurred and is worth the user's attention. If the change is not meaningful, there is no notification — silence is the default, and it is a feature, not a gap.
Read-only, advisory-only. No notification ever executes, rebalances, moves, or stages a trade, and none contains a "do this for me" affordance. The most action a notification offers is to open the linked recommendation. AlyInvest holds no custody and executes nothing; the proactive layer only informs.

10.1.1 Trigger ↔ What-Changed ↔ compared-to-previous mapping

10.2 Materiality Gating — the anti-noise layer

Between "the Engine computed a delta" and "the user gets pinged" sits a mandatory materiality gate. This is the single most important part of the system: it is what separates a professional advisor from a chatbot that reacts to every price wiggle.

Guardrail — no notification unless the change is meaningful. Ordinary daily price movement, intraday volatility, and unchanged routine corporate actions NEVER produce a notification. A candidate signal must clear its trigger-specific materiality threshold, survive dedup and cooldown, and pass a per-user relevance check before it can be emitted. When in doubt, the gate stays closed.

Gating stages (evaluated in order)

  1. Threshold test. The Engine computes the candidate delta and compares it to the trigger's materiality threshold (the "Example materiality threshold" column above). Diff-backed triggers are measured relative to the previous recommendation snapshot, never to an arbitrary calendar point, so gating aligns with What-Changed. The two memory-derived triggers (goal_drift, missed_contribution) are measured against goal targets and contribution habits instead.
  2. Band / regime test. For continuous metrics (risk, diversification, valuation), a crossing into a new qualitative band (risk band, valuation regime, HHI concentration tier) is treated as material even if the raw percentage move is modest. Movement within a band is not.
  3. Relevance test. The exposure filter: earnings, news, dividend, and valuation triggers only fire when the affected position is a material share of the portfolio (default ≥ 5%). A 0.4% position reporting earnings is not the user's problem.
  4. Cooldown & dedup test. The same underlying change may re-clear a threshold on consecutive days; a per-(user, trigger_type, ref_id) cooldown and a content hash prevent re-notifying about a change already communicated (see §10.4).
  5. Personalization test. Investment Memory can suppress or downgrade a trigger the user has repeatedly dismissed, and goal context can promote one (e.g. a user whose stated goal is income cares more about dividend_announcement).
def gate(signal: CandidateSignal, prev: Recommendation, memory: InvestmentMemory) -> GateResult:
    # 1. Threshold — diff-backed triggers use the previous recommendation snapshot;
    #    memory-derived triggers (goal_drift, missed_contribution) use goal/habit baselines.
    baseline = memory.baseline_for(signal.trigger_type, prev)
    if not signal.exceeds_threshold(baseline=baseline):
        return GateResult.suppress("below_threshold")

    # 2. Band / regime crossing counts as material even for small raw moves.
    material = signal.crossed_band(baseline=baseline) or signal.exceeds_threshold(baseline=baseline)
    if not material:
        return GateResult.suppress("no_band_change")

    # 3. Relevance — exposure filter for position-scoped triggers.
    if signal.is_position_scoped and signal.position_weight < 0.05:
        return GateResult.suppress("immaterial_exposure")

    # 4. Cooldown + dedup — same change must not be repeated.
    if cooldown_active(signal.user_id, signal.trigger_type, signal.ref_id) \
       or already_sent(signal.content_hash):
        return GateResult.suppress("deduped")

    # 5. Personalization — Memory can downgrade chronically dismissed triggers.
    severity = memory.adjust_severity(signal.trigger_type, signal.base_severity)
    if severity == Severity.MUTED:
        return GateResult.suppress("user_muted")

    return GateResult.emit(severity=severity)

10.3 Delivery — channels, cadence, quiet hours

A gated, emitted notification is persisted first (durability before delivery), then routed according to per-user preferences.

In-app

Always written. The notification center is the canonical record; every entry deep-links to its recommendation, What-Changed comparison, and Timeline entry. In-app never respects quiet hours — it is pull, not push.

Push

Opt-in per trigger-severity. Reserved for high severity and time-sensitive triggers (upcoming earnings, material news). Suppressed during quiet hours unless severity is critical.

Email

Best for digest content and long-form reasoning. The daily/weekly digest is delivered here; immediate emails are reserved for high+ severity to avoid inbox fatigue.

Digest vs. immediate

  • Immediate — time-sensitive, high-severity triggers (upcoming_earnings inside 48h, material_news, a risk-band jump). Delivered on the channels the user enabled for that severity, subject to quiet hours.
  • Digest — everything else is batched into a single daily (or weekly, per preference) roll-up so ten small changes become one calm summary rather than ten pings. The digest is assembled at the end of the daily pipeline from all pending non-immediate notifications.

Quiet hours & rate limits

  • Per-user quiet-hours window (default 21:00–08:00 local). Push/email held until the window closes; in-app unaffected. critical severity (e.g. a goal-threatening drift plus a risk-band jump) may override quiet hours if the user allows it.
  • A global per-user delivery cap (default: max 1 immediate push per 3 hours, remainder rolled into the digest) protects against a burst of correlated triggers on a volatile day.

10.4 Dedup — one change, one message

Dedup operates at two levels. A content hash over (trigger_type, ref_type, ref_id, materiality_bucket) collapses re-detections of the same underlying change; if the hash was already sent, the new candidate is suppressed. A cooldown per (user_id, trigger_type, ref_id) (default 7 days, shorter for earnings/news) prevents nagging while the same condition persists. If a condition worsens across a band boundary, the cooldown is bypassed because that is a genuinely new, more-material change — which is exactly what the user hired an advisor to catch.

Monthly RANGE partitioning is applied to the append-only, unbounded-growth series only: timeline_events, allocation_history, portfolio_health_history, risk_metric_history, and confidence_history (all owned by Memory/Timeline), plus market_snapshots and audit_log owned here. Each is partitioned on its time column (occurred_at / as_of / ts), which is included in that table's primary key. New partitions are pre-created by a scheduled job; cold partitions can be detached to cheaper storage without touching hot paths. Timeline partitions are never dropped — the timeline is permanent.
  • recommendations is deliberately NOT partitioned. It is the parent of many inbound foreign keys (recommendation_reasoning, recommendation_explanations, recommendation_news, recommendation_diffs, confidence_history, and the supersedes_id self-reference). Partitioning the parent would force every one of those FKs to reference the full composite key, so it keeps a simple PRIMARY KEY (id) and is indexed by (user_id, created_at DESC). This resolves the earlier three-way inconsistency: the Financial Engine, Timeline (6.6), and this section now all state that the recommendations spine is un-partitioned and only timeline_events (and the other history series) are partitioned.
  • Indexing convention. Composite indexes lead with the tenant key and end with the time column descending — (user_id, as_of DESC), (user_id, occurred_at DESC), (user_id, created_at DESC) — because every read is "latest N for this user".
  • Read replicas. The primary takes all writes (ingestion, engine snapshots, recommendation and audit inserts). All analytical and user-facing reads — timeline rendering, "What Changed?" diffs, memory review, dashboards — are routed to streaming read replicas. Ingestion and audit writes land on the primary and are read-your-writes consistent; replica lag is acceptable for the read-heavy advisory surfaces.

11.4 Structural proof: no order, no trade, no movement

There is no table in this schema — anywhere, in any section — that can represent an order, a trade, an execution, a transfer, a deposit, a withdrawal, or any movement of money or assets. The write surface is exhausted by: identity, read-only broker connections and their sync runs, ingested positions, reference instrument/market/news data, deterministic engine snapshots (portfolio_snapshots + metric_snapshots), durable memory, the permanent recommendation timeline, notifications, and the audit log. broker_connections.scopes is CHECK-constrained to the read-only whitelist, so a connection cannot even describe execution capability. AlyInvest never holds money, never executes, never moves assets, and never asks the user to move assets — the persistence layer makes that promise structurally, not merely by policy.
Section 12

API Surface (FastAPI)

The API is a thin, stateless FastAPI gateway. It authenticates the caller, resolves the user, orchestrates the deterministic Financial Engine and the persistence layer, and serializes results. It contains no financial mathematics: every numeric field it returns was computed by the engine and read back from Postgres or Redis. The gateway can refuse to serialize a payload whose numbers lack an engine provenance tag (see Numeric provenance below).

Hard invariant — no custody, no execution. There is no endpoint in this surface that places, modifies, or cancels an order; transfers cash or assets; or writes anything back to a brokerage. Broker connections are read-only data sources. Any attempt to add an execution path is an architectural violation, not a feature request. The API can advise; it can never act on the market.

12.1 Conventions

  • Transport & versioning. REST over HTTPS, JSON bodies (application/json), all paths prefixed /v1. Breaking changes ship under /v2; additive fields never break clients.
  • Auth. Supabase Auth / Clerk issues a short-lived JWT. The gateway verifies the JWT signature and aud/exp at the edge, extracts user_id, and injects it into a request-scoped context. No endpoint trusts a client-supplied user id. Auth column in the tables below: JWT = end-user token; Public = no auth; Service = internal service token (mTLS + signed token), never issued to browsers.
  • Pagination. Cursor-based. Requests take ?limit= (default 25, max 100) and ?cursor=; responses return { "data": [...], "page": { "next_cursor": "...", "has_more": true } }. Cursors are opaque, signed, and encode a stable sort key (e.g. (created_at DESC, id DESC)) so the permanent timeline paginates deterministically even as new entries are appended.
  • Idempotency. Every POST/PATCH/DELETE that mutates state requires an Idempotency-Key header (client-generated UUID). The gateway persists (user_id, idempotency_key) -> response for 24h; a replay returns the stored response with Idempotency-Replayed: true instead of re-executing. This protects against double-committed CSV imports, duplicate feedback, and retried disconnects.
  • Streaming. Two endpoints stream via Server-Sent Events (text/event-stream): the advisor answer (POST /v1/advisor/ask) and connection sync progress (POST /v1/connections/{id}/sync). Events are typed (event: token|progress|done|error).
  • Errors. RFC 9457 problem+json: { "type", "title", "status", "detail", "trace_id" }. The advisor never emits a raw stack trace; user-facing copy is written by the Advisor layer.
Numeric provenance. Every response object carrying a computed number includes an engine block: { "engine_version": "fe-2.14.0", "computed_at": "…", "inputs_hash": "…" }. The serializer asserts this block is present before returning; a payload with numbers but no provenance is a 500 missing_engine_provenance, never a silent guess. The LLM layers (GPT reasoning, Claude advisor prose) may reference these numbers but the gateway will reject any numeric value that did not arrive from the engine.

Step 5 — Risk Assessment

This is a professional behavioral questionnaire, explicitly not a single slider. A slider measures nothing; it just asks the user to guess a number. Instead we ask several situational questions whose answers reveal both risk tolerance (temperament) and risk capacity (ability to absorb loss), then score them into a numeric value and a band.

The anchor question is the drawdown scenario:

"Your portfolio falls 25% over three months. What do you do?"

  • A. Sell everything — I can't watch it keep falling. (most conservative)
  • B. Sell some to reduce the stress.
  • C. Do nothing and wait it out.
  • D. Buy more — it's on sale. (most aggressive)

Other items cover: reaction to volatility, time until the money is needed, income stability, prior experience with losses, and a trade-off question between smoother returns and higher expected growth. Each answer carries a weight; the score is a deterministic weighted sum computed by the Financial Engine — the LLM never scores the questionnaire.

RISK_WEIGHTS = {
    "drawdown_25pct":   {"A": 0,  "B": 8,  "C": 18, "D": 25},
    "volatility_feel":  {"A": 0,  "B": 6,  "C": 12, "D": 18},
    "time_horizon":     {"lt3": 0, "y3_7": 8, "y7_15": 14, "y15p": 20},
    "income_stability": {"unstable": 0, "moderate": 7, "stable": 12},
    "loss_experience":  {"never": 3, "sold_in_panic": 0, "held": 9, "bought_more": 12},
    "growth_vs_smooth": {"smooth": 0, "balanced": 7, "growth": 13},
}

def score_risk(answers: dict[str, str]) -> dict:
    raw = sum(RISK_WEIGHTS[q][answers[q]] for q in RISK_WEIGHTS)
    # normalize to 0-100
    max_raw = sum(max(opts.values()) for opts in RISK_WEIGHTS.values())
    score = round(100 * raw / max_raw)
    band = (
        "conservative" if score < 30 else
        "moderate"     if score < 55 else
        "growth"       if score < 78 else
        "aggressive"
    )
    return {"risk_score": score, "risk_band": band, "raw": raw, "max_raw": max_raw}

Crucially, the questionnaire is adaptive and keeps asking until confident. If a user's answers are internally contradictory — panic-sells in the drawdown scenario yet claims a 20-year horizon and picks maximum growth — the score sits near a band boundary with high disagreement. In that case we present one or two tie-breaking scenarios rather than forcing a coin-flip classification. Confidence is a first-class output stored alongside the score; a low-confidence risk band is flagged for the advisor to revisit as the relationship matures.

Section 15

Security, Compliance, Privacy & Scaling

The no-custody pivot is not merely a product decision; it is the single most important fact about AlyInvest's security and compliance posture. Because the system never holds client assets, never executes trades, never moves funds, and never asks a user to move assets, an entire category of custodial and broker-dealer risk simply does not apply to us. This section specifies how we turn that architectural constraint into a durable compliance advantage, and how we secure, observe, and scale the read-only advisory system that sits above the user's own brokerage.

Governing principle. AlyInvest is architecturally incapable of moving money or placing a trade. There is no code path, no credential scope, and no API surface anywhere in the system that can instruct a broker to buy, sell, or transfer. Every broker connection is read-only by construction. Security review can verify this as a structural property, not a policy promise.

15.1 No-Custody / Read-Only Posture as a Compliance Advantage

Custody, execution, and asset movement are what trigger the heaviest regulatory regimes — broker-dealer registration, custody rules, safeguarding-of-assets obligations, transaction reporting, and the associated capital and audit requirements. AlyInvest deliberately does none of these things. The user keeps investing through their existing broker (Trading 212, Interactive Brokers, Robinhood, eToro, Charles Schwab, Fidelity, Vanguard); we only analyze and advise.

ActivityBroker / CustodianAlyInvest AI
Holds client cash or securitiesYesNever
Executes / routes ordersYesNever
Moves or transfers assetsYesNever
Requests write/trade credentialsN/ANever — read-only scopes only
Reads positions to analyze—Yes (with user consent)
Produces personalized adviceSometimesYes — core product

Open compliance question (roadmap, per-jurisdiction). Automated personalized investment advice may implicate investment-adviser regulation independently of custody — e.g. SEC/state RIA registration in the US, MiFID II investment-advice rules in the EU, FCA rules in the UK. Removing custody narrows exposure dramatically but does not by itself exempt personalized advice. Before offering personalized recommendations in a given market we must obtain a jurisdiction-specific legal opinion and decide per market whether to (a) register/partner, (b) constrain output to educational/non-advisory framing, or (c) not launch. The engine already stamps every recommendation with the assumptions and reasoning needed for a suitability/audit trail, which supports either path. This is tracked as a launch-gating item per country, keyed to the user's declared residency.

15.2 Authentication & Authorization

Identity is delegated to Supabase Auth or Clerk (managed provider — we do not build password storage or MFA ourselves). Every request into the FastAPI layer carries a signed JWT verified at the gateway; the token subject (sub) is the canonical user_id. Authorization is enforced in three layers so that no single mistake exposes another user's data.

  1. Gateway: reject any request without a valid, unexpired, correctly-audienced JWT. MFA required for linking a new broker connection (a sensitive action).
  2. Service layer: every query is scoped by user_id from the verified token — never from a client-supplied body/param.
  3. Database — Postgres Row-Level Security (RLS) as defense-in-depth: even a service bug that forgets a WHERE user_id = ... cannot leak cross-user rows, because the database itself refuses.

15.3 Broker Token Security

Broker connections are the most sensitive credentials in the system, so they are handled with the strictest controls — and, critically, they are read-only by scope. When integrating any broker we request only read scopes (positions, transactions, account metadata) and never a trade, order, or transfer scope. If a broker's OAuth product does not offer a read-only tier, we fall back to CSV import or manual entry rather than accept broader access.

Least privilege, enforced twice. (1) We request read-only scopes at consent time. (2) The Financial Engine and integration layer expose no method that could place an order — there is no execute(), no place_order(), no transfer() anywhere in the codebase. Broker adapters implement a single read interface (see the Portfolio Connections section) and are addable without touching the engine.

Storage. Raw OAuth access/refresh tokens are never stored in the application database. They live in a dedicated secrets vault (e.g. AWS Secrets Manager / GCP Secret Manager / HashiCorp Vault), encrypted at rest under a KMS-managed key. The app DB stores only an opaque token_ref pointer plus non-secret metadata.

CREATE TABLE broker_connections (
  id            uuid PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id       uuid NOT NULL REFERENCES users(id),
  broker        text NOT NULL,                 -- 'trading212','ibkr','robinhood',...
  source_type   text NOT NULL,                 -- 'oauth' | 'csv' | 'manual'
  scopes        text[] NOT NULL,               -- read-only scopes ONLY; CHECK below
  token_ref     text,                          -- opaque vault path; NEVER the token
  status        text NOT NULL DEFAULT 'active',-- active|revoked|error|expired
  connected_at  timestamptz NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
  rotated_at    timestamptz,
  last_sync_at  timestamptz,
  CONSTRAINT no_write_scopes CHECK (
    NOT (scopes && ARRAY['trade','order','write','transfer','withdraw']::text[])
  )
);
  • Encrypted & rotated: refresh tokens rotated on schedule and on any anomaly; rotated_at tracked. Rotation touches only the vault; the app DB pointer is unchanged.
  • Revocable: a user can disconnect a broker at any time — we call the broker's revoke endpoint, delete the vault secret, and set status='revoked'. Analysis silently degrades to last-synced snapshot or CSV/manual data.
  • Isolated failure: a broker returning errors trips a per-broker circuit breaker (§15.7) without affecting other users or other brokers.

15.4 Data Privacy

The Investment Memory and Investment Timeline make AlyInvest valuable precisely because they persist for years — which raises the privacy stakes. We minimize, encrypt, localize, and provide a clean deletion path.

  • PII minimization. We store what advice requires (holdings, goals, risk profile, horizon, country, currency) and avoid what it does not. We do not need or store bank credentials, SSNs/national IDs, or full account numbers — broker linkage is via OAuth token_ref, not raw account secrets.
  • Encryption. TLS 1.2+ in transit everywhere (including internal service hops); AES-256 at rest for the database, backups, vault, and object storage. Field-level encryption for the most sensitive memory fields.
  • Data residency. The user declares country and currency at onboarding (also used for tax/FX context by the engine). We use this to route storage to the appropriate regional data store and to gate jurisdictional advice rules. Residency is a first-class attribute, not an afterthought.

Right to be forgotten

Account closure triggers a purge/anonymize job across the permanent memory and timeline. Because the timeline is the product's core value, deletion is careful and auditable rather than a naive DELETE.

def close_account(user_id: str) -> DeletionReceipt:
    revoke_all_broker_tokens(user_id)          # vault secrets destroyed first
    held = timeline_entries_under_legal_hold(user_id)   # tax/audit/legal retention
    # Purge everything not held; anonymize held rows (strip PII, keep aggregates)
    purge_memory(user_id, exclude=held)
    anonymize_timeline(user_id, keep_ids=held) # sever user linkage, retain lawful record
    purge_secondary(user_id)                    # caches, search indexes, backups (on cycle)
    return DeletionReceipt(user_id=user_id, purged_at=utcnow(),
                           held_count=len(held), reason="user_requested_closure")

Legal/audit-hold nuance. Some jurisdictions require retaining advice records (suitability/audit) for a fixed period even after account closure. Held entries are anonymized (user linkage severed, PII stripped) but retained as a lawful record until the hold expires, then purged on the next cycle. Backups age out per the backup retention policy; the deletion receipt records what was purged vs. held.

15.5 AI Governance

Three distinct components have three distinct trust levels, and the guardrails follow that split: the deterministic Python Financial Engine is the single source of truth for all numbers; GPT performs structured-JSON reasoning; Claude ("Your Advisor") writes the user-facing explanation. The LLM never computes a number.

Connectionsread-only broker / CSV / manual data (untrusted strings)
Financial Enginedeterministic; all numbers; source of truth
GPTstructured-JSON reasoning over engine output
Reconcileevery number must match the engine
Claudepremium plain-language advice

Prompt-injection defense

Untrusted text enters from three directions: news articles, user free text ("Ask Your Advisor"), and broker-sourced strings (instrument names, descriptions). All three are treated as data, never instructions.

  • Structural separation: untrusted content is passed in clearly delimited data fields, never concatenated into the instruction section of the prompt. System instructions explicitly state that content inside data delimiters is information to analyze, not commands to obey.
  • Sanitization: strip/neutralize instruction-like patterns from broker and news strings before they reach any model.
  • Capability isolation: even a successful injection can do no financial harm — the model has no tool that trades, moves money, or writes authoritative numbers. The blast radius of injection is limited to text, and text is validated (below).

Output validation & numeric reconciliation

Hard gate. Every numeric value appearing in a GPT or Claude output must correspond exactly to a value emitted by the Financial Engine for that recommendation. Reconciliation is a blocking check: if the advisor text contains a number the engine did not produce, the output is rejected and regenerated, and the discrepancy is logged as an incident. The LLM is never permitted to introduce, round, or invent a figure.

def reconcile(advisor_text: str, engine_facts: dict[str, float]) -> None:
    allowed = {canonical(v) for v in engine_facts.values()}
    for num in extract_numeric_tokens(advisor_text):   # %, $, ratios, scores
        if canonical(num) not in allowed:
            raise NumericReconciliationError(token=num, allowed=allowed)
    # Guardrail phrases: block promised returns / asset-movement suggestions
    for bad in FORBIDDEN_PHRASES:                       # "guaranteed", "will return",
        if bad in normalize(advisor_text):              # "move your money to", "sell now"
            raise ContentGuardrailError(phrase=bad)

Content guardrails

  • Never promise returns. Returns are always framed as probabilistic scenarios with assumptions, never as guarantees. Forbidden-phrase and classifier checks block "guaranteed", "will earn", fixed-return language.
  • Never suggest moving assets. The advisor analyzes and advises on the user's existing portfolio; it never tells a user to move money, switch broker, or transfer assets. Language implying custody or execution by AlyInvest is blocked.
  • Distinguish fact / analysis / assumption / uncertainty. Output structure requires the advisor to separate what is measured (engine facts), what is inferred (analysis), what is assumed (assumptions), and what is unknown (uncertainty) — matching the timeline entry schema.

Model stamping, reproducibility & red-team evals

Every timeline entry stores the exact provenance needed to reproduce and audit a recommendation later.

15.6 Observability

The end-to-end pipeline — connections → engine → GPT → Claude — is fully traced so any recommendation can be reconstructed and any failure localized.

  • Structured logging (JSON) with a trace_id propagated across every stage; logs redact PII and never contain raw tokens.
  • Distributed tracing spans each stage; a single recommendation's trace shows sync latency, engine compute, GPT call, reconciliation, and Claude call.
  • Metrics: pipeline latency and success rate, reconciliation-rejection rate, guardrail-trip rate, notification precision (were "meaningful change" alerts actually acted on).
  • Per-AI-call cost is recorded per request (tokens in/out × model price) and rolled up per user and per feature — cost is a first-class metric because it drives unit economics at scale.
  • Data-quality & sync-health monitoring: stale-price detection, missing-holding detection, per-broker sync success rate, CSV parse-error rate; a data-quality failure downgrades confidence and can suppress advice rather than advise on bad data.
  • Alerting on reconciliation-rejection spikes, guardrail trips, broker sync failures, cost anomalies, and pipeline latency SLO breaches.

15.7 Reliability

Graceful degradation to engine-only output. Because the deterministic engine is the source of truth, the product still functions when the LLMs are unavailable: we serve the engine's structured facts and a templated, non-generated explanation, clearly marked as a reduced-detail advisory. We never fabricate numbers to fill a gap, and we never block the user from seeing their engine-computed portfolio health.

  • Fallback models/providers: if the primary advisor model is degraded, fail over to a secondary model/provider that passes the same reconciliation + guardrail gates; if none is available, drop to engine-only.
  • Per-broker circuit breakers: a broker that is slow or erroring is tripped open, isolating it so its failures never cascade; that user's analysis uses the last good snapshot with reduced confidence and a "data may be stale" flag.
  • Idempotent, retryable pipeline: daily runs and syncs are safe to retry; partial failures resume without duplicating timeline entries.

15.8 Scaling to Millions

The hardest scaling reality is that the Investment Memory and Timeline grow forever — every user accumulates a permanent daily record. The architecture is built for that from day one.

Stateless API

FastAPI instances hold no session state; auth is JWT, data is in Postgres/Redis. Horizontal scale is trivial behind a load balancer.

Worker fan-out

The daily recommendation pipeline and broker syncs run as queued jobs fanned out across a worker pool — one job per user/portfolio — not in the request path.

Queue backpressure

Bounded queues with backpressure and rate-limited broker calls prevent thundering-herd syncs and respect broker API quotas.

Caching tiers

Redis for hot portfolio snapshots and market data; recommendation outputs cached until inputs change (the "What Changed?" comparison naturally keys on input deltas).

Time-partitioned history

Timeline/memory tables partitioned by month; old partitions move to cheaper storage. Reads hit recent partitions; the ever-growing tail stays cheap.

Read replicas

Timeline/memory reads (which dominate at scale) are served from replicas; the primary handles writes from the daily pipeline.

AI cost control

  • Comparison-based generation: the daily "What Changed?" flow compares against the prior recommendation and only invokes the full LLM path when a meaningful change occurred — most days are cheap diffs, not full regenerations.
  • Prompt caching & tiering: cache stable prompt prefixes; route simple explanations to smaller/cheaper models and reserve the premium advisor model for substantive advice.
  • Notification thresholds double as cost control: proactive alerts fire only on meaningful changes, so we do not pay to regenerate advice nothing changed for.
  • Per-user cost budgets with alerting; if a user's automated cost exceeds budget, non-essential regenerations are throttled while engine output remains fully available.

Open questions flagged for the team. (1) Per-jurisdiction investment-adviser status and the exact advisory/educational framing per market. (2) Statutory advice-record retention periods that override right-to-be-forgotten, per country. (3) Which brokers offer genuinely read-only OAuth tiers vs. CSV-only fallback. (4) Data-residency obligations that may force regional database isolation for specific markets. Each is launch-gating for the affected market and should be resolved with counsel before that market goes live.

def is_material(prev: Health, curr: Health, thresholds: Thresholds) -> list[str]:
    """Gate notifications. No entry -> no notification. Numbers from the engine only."""
    reasons = []
    if curr.hhi > thresholds.hhi_alert and curr.hhi - prev.hhi > thresholds.hhi_delta:
        reasons.append("concentration_rising")
    if curr.vol_annual - prev.vol_annual > thresholds.vol_delta:
        reasons.append("risk_increasing")
    if prev.diversification - curr.diversification > thresholds.div_delta:
        reasons.append("diversification_falling")
    if curr.max_position_move_pct > thresholds.valuation_move:
        reasons.append("large_valuation_change")
